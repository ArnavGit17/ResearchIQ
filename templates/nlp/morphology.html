Inflectional Morphology

Inflection changes a word's grammatical properties (tense, number, person) without changing its core meaning or part of speech.

  • Root: play plays
    Third-person singular present (-s)
  • Root: play played
    Past tense / Past participle (-ed)
  • Root: study studies
    Plural / Third-person (y → ies)
Derivational Morphology

Derivation creates a new word, often changing its part of speech or core meaning entirely.

  • Base: teach (Verb) teacher (Noun)
    Agentive noun derivation (-er)
  • Base: happy (Adj) happiness (Noun)
    State noun derivation (y → i + ness)
  • Base: act (Verb) action (Noun)
    Action noun derivation (-ion)
